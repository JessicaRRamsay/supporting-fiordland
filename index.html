Welcome to Fiordland Fiordland is part of one of New Zealands three UNESCO World Heritage sites, and for a good reason. It is the largest national park in New Zealand, with many popular hiking tracks, cultural significance and has a rich biodiversity filled with glacier origins (making steep cliffs, fiords and vast landscapes from where past glaciers lay), rare species (including birds and marine life), freshwater lakes and rivers and a range of native plants and trees.
