Discover Fiordland

Fiordland is a hidden gem in the South West of New Zealand's South Island. Its 12, 500km2 making it New Zealandss biggest national park. It's known for having many good hikes, being a home to many of New Zealands native birds and marine life, as well as having a vast landscape.

Hiking Trails

Fiordland is a hikers paradise, it has a range of trails with breathtaking landscapes and views.
Some of the popular tracks are:

  • The Kepler Track, which is one of the great walks of New Zealalnd
  • The Milford Track, this is also one of the great walks
  • The Routeburn
  • Lake Marian Track
  • The Gertrude Saddle Route
  • The Key Summit Track

Native Birds

Fiordland is home to many of New Zelands native birds.
You can spot lots of species including:

  • Kea
  • Takahae
  • Kakapo
  • Kiwi
  • Kakariki
  • Whio
  • Weka

Marine Life

Fiordland also has an array of marine life, the fiords are home to many species.
Some of these species are:

  • Bottlenose dolphins
  • New Zealand fur seals
  • Fiordland crested penguins
  • Little blue penguins

Another thing that can be found in the fiords are Black coral trees, some of which are over 300 years old, with bristlestars intretwined with the black coral. Fiordland is also home to a branch of brachiopods, which are clamlike animals which is an ancient species that have existed for over 500 million years.

Landscapes

Fiordland has a range of landscapes, with special elements like serne waters and rugged coastline.
Some of the landscapes are:

  • Fiords, with towering cliffs and deep waters, which are carved by where glaciers used to lay. Some well known fiords are Milford Sound, Doubtful Sound, and Dusky Sound.
  • Mountains, The Southern Alps surruond the region, with snow capped peaks and rugged ranges, creating a stunning backdrop
  • Glacial Valleys, Fiordland has many U-shaped valleys which were carved by ancient glaciers, creating unique and striking formations
  • Rainforests, Dense temperate rainforests cover the area, filled with moss-covered trees, ferns, and native flora
  • Waterfalls, there are many spectacular waterfalls in Fiordland, such as Stirling Falls and Bowen Falls
  • Lakes, the area is filled with many serene lakes, making a stunning view, some of the well known lakes are, Lake Te Anau and Lake Manapouri
  • Coastline, Fiordland is know for its rugged coastline, which offers secluded beaches and dramatic cliffs meeting the Tasman Sea
