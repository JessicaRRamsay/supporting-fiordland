Support Fiordland

Although it may seem like there is nothing we can do to help... There is! Some ways in which we can help to protect Fiordland national park are through comunity led projects, involvement in invasive speices management, resposible tourism, sustainable practices, awareness, financial support, and respecting culture.

Community Led Projects

These projects can help you to give aid to what needs it most, and offers opurtunities to volenteer.

  • Participate on local restoration programs
  • Join native tree planting programs, this helps by getting more native trees in the area, helping to make it closer to what it used to be
  • Support predator control efforts, this could be setting up traps for pests, to lower their numbers and prevent them from killing birds and native species
  • Volenteer for habitat rehabilitation efforts

Invasive Species Management

Managing the amount of pests and invase species is important because these pests kill many native birds, and also eat their eggs too, which causes New Zealands bird numbers to decrease significantly. Some ways you can be involved in this are:

  • Learning to identify and report pests/invasive species
  • Participate in pest control organisations
  • Support local trapping initiatives
  • Practice responisble outdoor behaviour to prevent species spread

Responsible Tourism

If you ever get the chance to go to Fiordland, it is important that you are respectful to the whenua. Some ways you can do this is:

  • Staying on the trail, this means not walking over the mosses and bushes which prevents them from dying
  • Follow the 'Leave no trace' rule, this means you are not leaving rubbish behind you and being clean
  • Respect the wildlife and cultural areas
  • Use environmentally friendly tour operators

Sustainable Practices

Doing sustainable everyday contributions can considerably help, some of these could be:

  • Reducing your carbon footprint, this could be taking a bus rather then a car, using energy-efficent light bulbs, and avoiding food waste
  • Support local conservation organizations
  • Choose sustainable and locally-produced products
  • Learn about Fiordland's ecological importance

Awareness

By learning and helping others to learn more about Fiordland and the complecations to its biodiversity, ecosystems and nature you learn about more ways to help, and be respectful and support the enviroment. Some ways to learn more are:

  • Attend local conservation workshops
  • Participate in guided nature tours
  • Support educational programs about Fiordland's ecosystem
  • Share your knowledge with friends and family
  • Follow and support local conservation social media accounts

Financial support

Many conservation groups work very hard to support and care for the enviroment, but wihtout funding there is only so much that they can do. Donating and supporting these groups help them to make more of a positive impact as they are able to have more resources (this could be more trees to plant, more traps etc).

Unsplash, Josh Appel

Respecting the culture

It is important to respect and acknowledge the culture and traditions of the people who came first, in Māori culture the land and nature are very important and are to be respected and treated well. This means practicing Kaitiakitanga, which is a Māori value about guardianship and care for the enviroment which embodies protecting the land, which is important for keeping Fiordland the way it is, allowing its natrual elements and beauty to come through!
