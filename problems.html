Challenges to Fiordland

Fiordland National Park has been negativly impacted by many factors, which are either in one or both of two main groups: Enviromental and Human caused issues. These factors can negativly affect the biodiversity, species and flora in Fiordland.

Climate Change

One of the main causes for impacts is climate change. Rising air and water temperatures, changing conditions and extreme weather can cause some of the follow effects:

  • Glaciers retreating, this is caused by rising temperatures
  • Biodiversity shifts, this disrupts the various ecosystems and vegetation, as it could be too hot making it harder for it to survive, this also affects the native species.
  • Ocean acidfication, caused by the ocean absobing excess carbon dioxide. This impacts marine life, particually species which rely on calcium cabonate (creatures with shells and coral) as the acidifcation affects the avalibility of the calcium. Another affect is on smaller organisms (including plankton), with further impacts the food web and larger species, marine animals also spend more energy to neutralize the affect of the acidifcation.

Invasive Species

Invasive speices include pests (like stoats, rats and possums), invasive plants (weeds) and invasive marine species which compete with native flora and fauna and take up their resources. These species risk disrupting the fragile ecosystems in Fiordland, and kill many native species. There is also a threat of new pest being introduced which could kill many more native species. With increased golbsl conectivity there are more people moving in and out of New Zealand, this raises biosecurity concerns about new species being introduced which could harm Fiordland and New Zealands rich biodiversity.

Biodiversity Loss

Fiordland has a rich biodiversity, which faces many challenges including:

  • Declining population of endemic and native species
  • Reduced genetic diversity
  • Habitat fragmentation
  • Distruption to cological relationships

Tourism Impacts

While tourism brings awareness and economic benefits, it also creates environmental stress and causes negative impacts includig:

  • Increased foot traffic damaging sensitive ecosystems
  • Carbon emissions from transportation
  • Litter being left on the national park and waste management challenges

Resource Management

Resource management means sustainably using resources, not over-using resources so that there can be plenty of that resource which can used in the future. Some areas/industries where resources which need to be carefully and sustainably used are:

  • Fishing industry, not overfishing which could impact the marine biodiversity of Fiordland
  • Potential mining interests, if mining operations are started near Fiordland this could create habitat destruction, pollution and long term impacts on Fiordland's biodiversity
